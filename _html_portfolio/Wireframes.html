/01 · Foundations · Voice

/01.1 Design Principles

  • Structure before aesthetics. Hierarchy first, polish second
  • Earn every element. No filler, no slop, no decorative noise
  • Type carries the weight. Imagery supports — never compensates
  • One accent only. Color signals state, not style
  • Numbered, not bulleted. Index everything · /01 · /02 · /03
  • Mono for metadata. Sans for ideas, mono for measurements

/01.2 Voice & Tone

English. Senior, direct, never marketing-y. German UI conventions translated into Swiss-modern English.

Do

“Single sign-on, single portal. Support tickets down 34%.”

Don't

“I had the privilege to leverage cross-functional synergies and ideate truly delightful experiences …”

Rules
  • Active voice · past tense for case studies
  • Numbers as digits (6 years · 23 issues · 89%)
  • Em dash for asides — sparingly
  • No emoji · no exclamation marks

/06 · Motion & Interaction

/06.1 Timing

  • Hover · 150 ms ease-out
  • Tab switch · 250 ms ease
  • Image fade in scroll · 350 ms
  • Page transition · none · honest reloads

/06.2 Primary Patterns

  • Row hover · 4px slide right + bg lightens
  • Arrow nudge · 4px translate on hover
  • Sticky scroll · image swaps with fade
  • Underline grow · text links · left-to-right

/06.3 Don't

  • No parallax
  • No bounce / spring easing
  • No entrance animations on scroll-in
  • No modals · use real pages
  • No autoplay video

Case Study · C — Decision Log

Problem → Decision → Outcome · Narrative cards · Senior-thinking on display
Argument-driven Short Most senior-feeling
Page Sketch · Card Sequence
Case Hero · Index-Traub · Machine ManagementMust
· Client · Index-Traub · Role · Lead UX & Workshop Lead · Year · 2023

A platform for managing global machine fleets — registered installs, service contracts, spare parts, technician access. The interesting part wasn't the screens; it was the five decisions we had to argue our way through.

Five Decisions
/01 Group by machine, not by order IA
Problem

Existing SAP backend organized everything around order numbers. Users (Service Technicians) think in physical machines, not orders.

Decision

Anchor the new IA on machine identity. Orders become a sub-view per machine. Argued through three reviews with PO and SAP architects.

IA before / after
Outcome

Service Technicians completed lookup tasks in 41% less time during testing. Backend mapping handled via a thin frontend service.

/02 Single dashboard, three roles Personas
Problem

Three distinct personas — Buyer, Admin, Technician — but the client wanted one platform, one URL, one onboarding.

Decision

Single dashboard, role-shaped widgets. The same component renders different data per role; layout adapts via grid order, not separate pages.

3 dashboard states
Outcome

Reduced build cost (one frontend) and let users who switch roles (Admin who is also a Buyer) stay in one mental model.

/03 No empty state for empty fleets Copy
Problem

New customers signed in to an empty dashboard. Test users assumed the system was broken.

Decision

Replaced "empty state" with a first-time setup checklist tied to onboarding workshops. Activation moves from passive to guided.

Empty → checklist
Outcome

First-week activation up. Trained sales-engineers report fewer "is this working?" calls in week one.

/04 Keep Balsamiq lo-fi all the way through testing Process
Problem

Pressure to skip wireframing and jump to Sketch hi-fi — "the client wants to see something real".

Decision

Held the line: usability tests on lo-fi wireframes, not on hi-fi. Users critique structure, not color. Three review rounds before pixel work.

Outcome

Saved an estimated 4 weeks of hi-fi rework. Stakeholders learned to trust the lo-fi → test → hi-fi sequence.

/05 Embed UX in the Scrum team, don't toss-over Delivery
Problem

Internal + external devs. Risk of design intent being lost in translation between two organizations across time zones.

Decision

Attended every standup, every refinement, every review. Set up a Figma component spec doc that both teams could comment on directly.

Outcome

Zero design-vs-build disputes through acceptance. PO described handover as "the smoothest we've had".

Why decision log

This is the pattern most read as senior. It shows you can argue, not just produce. Problem → Decision → Outcome mirrors how engineering ADRs work.

Anatomy
  • · 4–6 cards per case · no more
  • · Each card · 3 columns · same headers every time
  • · One artifact image per card · in the middle column
  • · Tag in top right · IA, Copy, Personas, Process, Delivery …
When to skip

If the project's value was volume of artifacts (lots of screens, big design system) — Sticky Scroll or Timeline carry that better.

Use this for

Mid-length, conceptually rich projects · Index-Traub · Mapal configurator · Titan Wagner CPQ

Recommendation

Mix patterns across your 4–5 cases. Sticky Scroll for your flagship, Timeline for the multi-quarter one, Decision Log for the rest.
