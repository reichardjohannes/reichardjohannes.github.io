Work / Geiger / Sales Rep Platform
Case Study · 2025 – 2026

Redesigning the Sales Rep experience for the world's largest promo company.

Geiger, the largest family-owned promotional products company in the US, needed a complete redesign of their Sales Rep application. Built on SAP Commerce Cloud, the project demanded extensive user research with internal stakeholders before translating complex B2B configurations into a streamlined, budget-conscious interface.

Challenge

Complex configurations in a rigid SAP context

Sales Reps needed to configure decorated promotional products (embroidery, imprints, proofs) with pricing, margin calculations and variant management all within SAP Commerce Cloud constraints. The legacy tool was slow and unintuitive.

My role

Research through handoff. Full UX ownership

Responsible for all UX deliverables: stakeholder workshops, user interviews, journey mapping, wireframes, UI design, client alignment and the creation of a comprehensive style guide for developer handoff.

Outcome

A platform Sales Reps actually want to use

A redesigned Sales Rep application that simplifies product configuration, surfaces margin data in real-time, and was delivered within budget, meeting both Geiger's business goals and their users' daily needs.

/01 · User Research

Understanding Sales Reps before touching a single screen.

Geiger prioritised a deep research phase from day one. We conducted stakeholder interviews and workshops with internal Sales Reps, product managers and technical leads to map out pain points, workflows and unmet needs.

The resulting user journey revealed where the legacy application forced workarounds, particularly around product configuration, decoration methods and margin visibility. These findings became the evidence base for every design decision.

/02 · Information Architecture

Structuring a complex B2B application from the ground up.

From the research insights, we defined the full sitemap and information architecture. The application needed to serve multiple workflows: product search, configuration, cart management, quoting, checkout and reporting, all within the SAP Commerce Cloud framework.

Key decisions at this stage: separating standard and non-standard composable storefront pages, defining the cart-to-quote flow, and mapping how configuration data flows between product detail, decorator selection and proof management.

/03 · UI Design

Translating research into a polished product interface.

The UI design phase translated wireframes and validated flows into high-fidelity screens. We designed the complete product listing with advanced faceted filtering (vendors, colours, sizes, price ranges — optimised for the way Sales Reps actually browse and sell.

Every screen went through multiple alignment rounds with Geiger to ensure it met both their brand standards and their Sales Reps' daily workflow needs. The dark header with Geiger's teal brand colour anchored the identity throughout.

/04 · Complex Workflows

Making margin, configuration and proofs transparent.

The cart and proposal view was the most complex screen in the application. Sales Reps needed to see variant breakdowns, decoration charges, margin percentages and total pricing, all at a glance, without navigating away.

We designed a configuration summary with real-time margin calculation, inline variant editing, and a streamlined proof management flow (PDF proofs, virtual proofs, sample proofs). The challenge: fitting this complexity into a clean, scannable layout within SAP's component constraints.

/05 · Style Guide & Handoff

From design to development. A complete style guide.

The final phase produced a comprehensive style guide for the development team: component specifications, spacing tokens, colour definitions, interaction patterns and responsive behaviour, all documented to ensure consistent implementation on SAP Commerce Cloud.

The handoff bridged the gap between Geiger's custom requirements and the SAP Composable Storefront's standard capabilities. Every custom adaptation was documented with rationale and technical constraints, so developers could implement without guesswork.

/06 · Reflections

What I'd carry forward.

Notes to self
for the next B2B platform

What worked

  • Research-first approach: Geiger's insistence on a thorough research phase paid off. Every design decision had evidence behind it, which shortened review cycles and reduced rework.
  • User journey as alignment tool: The journey map became the single artefact everyone referenced. Stakeholders, designers and developers could point to the same document when discussing scope or priorities.
  • SAP-aware design: Designing with SAP Commerce Cloud constraints in mind from the start avoided costly "that's not possible" moments later in development.

What I'd change

  • Earlier dev involvement: Some SAP Composable Storefront limitations only surfaced during implementation. Having a developer in the room during wireframe reviews would have caught these sooner.
  • More usability testing rounds: The research phase was strong, but we could have validated the configuration flow with real Sales Reps before moving to Hi-Fi, especially the decoration and proof management screens.
  • Sustainability integration: Geiger's CSR focus is a key differentiator. Surfacing sustainability data (eco-friendly materials, certifications) more prominently in the product listing would strengthen their brand story.
