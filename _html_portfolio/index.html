/02 · Approach

Phase 0 → Delivery.
The whole path.

I own the full UX path: client communication, research, workshops, wireframes, testing, and Scrum delivery with internal & external dev teams. Scroll through the six phases below.

01 / 06
Phase 0 · Alignment

Get everyone on the same page first.

Kickoff workshop with PO, PL and key stakeholders. We clarify the big picture: project scope, timeline, success metrics, and align on how the project will run. Who owns what, how we communicate, what the milestones look like.

A shared understanding at the start saves time later. This is where the foundation for smooth collaboration is set.

Output · Stakeholder map · Success metrics · Working agreement
/01 · Research & Audit

Understand who the users actually are.

Heuristic evaluation of existing systems. User interviews across primary personas, typically Buyer, Admin, Technician. The raw findings are synthesised into pain-point maps and user-journey maps, distilling the most actionable insights to guide every decision downstream.

Output · Audit doc · Personas · Journey maps
/02 · Wireframes & IA

Structure before aesthetics.

Lo-fi wireframes in Balsamiq. Iterative review rounds with the PO before pixel work begins. Sitemap usually collapses to half the legacy size. Most savings come from merging duplicate flows.

Every decision argued, never decorative. The point is to ship the right structure, not the prettiest one.

Output · Sitemap · Wireframes v1 / v2 / v3
/03 · Testing

Validate, don't assume.

Moderated usability tests with real users on wireframes and lo-fi prototypes. Findings feed straight into the next iteration loop, so every design decision is grounded in evidence, not guesswork.

Output · Test protocols · Findings deck
/04 · Hi-Fi & Component Library

From research to final design.

Final design in Sketch or Figma, grounded in the preceding research phases. Built around client-specific design guidelines, CI/CD, and technical requirements, respecting platform conventions and commerce-project constraints.

Close alignment with developers ensures feasibility throughout the project.

Output · Hi-Fi screens · Component spec
/05 · Scrum Delivery

Embedded UX through release.

Close collaboration with developers in an agile Scrum process, tailored to each project's needs. Design and development move in lockstep, eliminating redundant alignment rounds and keeping the project on track.

The result: faster delivery without wasting time or budget on avoidable miscommunication.

Output · Shipping product · Live since launch
