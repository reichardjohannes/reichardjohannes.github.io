/02 · Bio

From agency floor to senior consultant.

My focus sits in eCommerce, SAP Commerce, customer portals, fleet and machine management platforms. I work best in interdisciplinary teams alongside internal and external developers, where strong communication is the difference between a good design and one that actually ships.

I own the whole UX path: client communication, research, workshops, wireframes, testing, alignment, and delivery. I speak the language of PO/PL stakeholders just as well as the dev team on the other side of the handover.

I also run internal UX training sessions — presenting what user-centred design actually means and why it matters, especially for developers, project leads and product owners. The goal: put the user front and centre across every role in a project.

AI is changing how we design, and I lean into that. I use it across the entire workflow, from research synthesis to prototyping to delivery. Not as a shortcut, but as a tool that makes every phase faster and sharper. Better iterations, better insights, better outcomes for the team and the end user.

/03 · Process

A human-centred design process.

Every decision starts from a real user need — observed, not assumed. These six phases are how that principle becomes concrete work: from alignment and research through testing to delivery. Skip the user, and you ship guesswork.

Iterative process
01 Plan the UX process
02 Understand the context of use
03 Specify the requirements
04 Design concept & solutions
05 Evaluate against requirements
ISO 9241-210 — the cycle repeats until the design meets user requirements.

/04 · User Research

Before the first pixel: understand the user.

01
Contextual Inquiry · Shadowing
A day in their shoes
Output · Context map · Field notes

Observe people in their real environment — the tools, the interruptions, the workarounds they never mention in a meeting.

Possible insights
  • Real workflow ≠ documented process
  • Hidden workarounds and silent frustrations
02
Qualitative Interviews
Listening, one to one
Output · Transcripts · Affinity diagram

Open, non-leading questions about their day, goals and pain points. The point isn't answers — it's the “why” underneath them.

Possible insights
  • Motivations and mental models
  • The language users actually use
03
Synthesis · Affinity Mapping
Empathy map
Output · Empathy map · Clusters

Cluster what we heard into says · thinks · does · feels — and the contradictions between them become impossible to ignore.

Possible insights
  • Emotional drivers behind decisions
  • Say–do gaps worth designing around
04
Segmentation · Persona Sheets
Persona sheets
Output · 2–4 persona sheets

Consolidate the patterns into a handful of personas — goals, frustrations, context — one shared reference the whole team designs against.

Possible insights
  • A shared language across the team
  • Prioritised, real user needs
05
Scenarios · Journey Mapping
Scenarios & journey map
Output · Journey map · Scenarios

Map tasks and touchpoints with the emotional curve across the journey — the dips show exactly where the experience breaks.

Possible insights
  • Pain points located on the timeline
  • Opportunity moments to design for
